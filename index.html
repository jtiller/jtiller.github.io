ASE 374L - Spacecraft/Mission Design Spring 2019

The purpose of this mission is to retrieve a soil sample from the Aitken Basin and characterize the surrounding environment. This satisfies a need outlined in the decadal survey indicating a desire for a deeper knowledge of the formation of the solar system which can be obtained in a cost effective manner from the lunar south pole.

To The Moon

Atlas V Launch Vehicle

With a launch success rate of 98.7% and capable of 2.4 million pounds of force, this launch vehicle will easily get us to the moon.

Orbiter Relay

L-SPIDER will communicate to Earth via a lunar orbiter and will return the lunar sample to the orbiter instead of Earth, consequently increasing its payload capacity.

Science

Over 1kg of Lunar soil will be collected, along with environment measurments such as seismic data, atmospheric composition, and radiation just to name a few.

Return to Earth

History will be made as the first Lunar South Pole sample to be brought back to Earth.
